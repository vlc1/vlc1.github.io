Isothermal, isochoric irreversible kinetics

We consider here one of the simplest physical examples of isothermal, isochoric kinetics : O-O2 dissociation and recombination. The N=2 compounds are

O(i=1)

and

O2(i=2).

The J=2 reactions are

O+O+M→O2+M(j=1),

O2+M→O+O+M(j=2).

The irreversible reaction mechanism presented in class (found here) provides :

a1=2.90×1017(molcm3)−2Ks,β1=−1,E¯1=0kJmol

and

a2=6.81×1018(molcm3)−1Ks,β2=−1,E¯2=496.41kJmol.

Finally, the collision efficiencies are given as f1=1 and f2=0.35.

The temporal evolution of the O and O2 molar concentrations is given by :

{dρ¯1dt=−2r1(ρ¯1,ρ¯2,T)+2r2(ρ¯1,ρ¯2,T),dρ¯2dt=r1(ρ¯1,ρ¯2,T)−r2(ρ¯1,ρ¯2,T)

where

{r1(ρ¯1,ρ¯2,T)=ρ¯1ρ¯1ρ¯Mk1(T),r2(ρ¯1,ρ¯2,T)=ρ¯2ρ¯Mk2(T)

and

ρ¯M=fiρ¯i,

Isothermal, isochoric reversible kinetics

We now model this system with a single (J=1) reversible reaction :

O2+M⇋O+O+M.

Here,

a=1.85×1011(molcm3)−1K−0.5s,β=0.5,E¯=400kJmol.

The evolution equations are now given by

{dρ¯1dt=2r(ρ¯1,ρ¯2,T),dρ¯2dt=−r(ρ¯1,ρ¯2,T)

where

r(ρ¯1,ρ¯2,T)=k(T)(ρ¯2ρ¯M−1Kc(T)ρ¯1ρ¯1ρ¯M)

and

ρ¯M=ρ¯1+ρ¯2,

k(T)=aTβexp⁡(−E/R¯T).

The equilibrium constant is given as a function of temperature

Kc(T)=PoR¯Texp⁡(−ΔrGo(T)R¯T)

where the standard enthalpy of reaction is given as

ΔrGo(T)=2G¯1o(T)−G¯2o(T)

and Po=1Pa denotes the standard pressure.

NASA 9-coefficient polynomial parameterization

The NASA 9-coefficient polynomial parameterization is used to compute reference-state thermodynamic properties C¯po(T), H¯o(T), S¯o(T) and G¯o(T).

Definitions

The heat capacity at constant pressure is given as :

C¯p,io(T)R¯=a1T2+a2T+a3+a4T+a5T2+a6T3+a7T4.

These coefficient also provide the "engineering enthalpy", defined as

H¯io(T)=ΔfH¯io(298,15)+∫298,15TC¯p,io(T^)dT^

by the following parameterization

H¯io(T)R¯T=−a1T2+a2ln⁡TT+a3+a4T2+a5T23+a6T34+a7T45+b1T.

Finally,

S¯io(T)R¯=−a12T2−a2T+a3ln⁡T+a4T+a5T22+a6T33+a7T44+b2.

Likewise, Gibbs' free energy can be obtained from

G¯io(T)R¯T=H¯io(T)R¯T−S¯io(T)R¯,=−a12T2+2a2(1−ln⁡T)T+a3(1−ln⁡T)−a4T2−a5T26−a6T312−a7T420+b1T−b2.

ρ¯C¯VdTdt=−rΔrEo(T)

where ρ¯ and C¯V denote the molar concentration and heat capacity at constant volume of the mixture, while

ΔrEo(T)=2E¯2o(T)−E¯1o(T).
